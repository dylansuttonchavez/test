Neural networks are computational models inspired by the human brain, capable of learning complex patterns from data through interconnected layers of nodes.

A neural network consists of layers of interconnected neurons, where each connection carries a weight that determines the strength of the signal. When data is passed through the network, each neuron applies a mathematical function to its inputs, transforms the data, and passes it to the next layer. This process allows the network to learn and adapt by adjusting weights based on the error between predicted and actual outcomes during training.
